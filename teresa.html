A biography

Mother Teresa was a missionery when worked among the poor people of Calcutta, India.

She born in Macedonia on 26 August 1910. Her father who was Albanian died when she was just eight years old.

Her mother left alone to bring up the family.

Agnes left home in September 1928. She had wanted becoming a missionary from a very young age, so she joined a convent in Ireland.

She was given the name Teresa, and in 1929 she was sent to St Mary's High School Convent in Calcutta, which she worked as a teacher.

She still was working there in 1937, as Mother Teresa.
